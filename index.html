About

I’m a Computer Science Engineering student and a CS engineer in progress, interested in system-level work and real-world problem solving. I focus on building software and systems that are reliable, understandable, and designed with long-term stability in mind.

I enjoy working on problems that require logic and careful reasoning rather than guesswork. My process usually starts away from the keyboard— using pen and paper to simulate behavior, relate problems to real-world constraints, and break them down into smaller, manageable parts before implementation.

I value reliability and simplicity above everything else. I prefer systems that behave predictably, are easy to reason about, and work the way they are intended to.

My interests lean strongly toward system-level engineering. I work with Linux and Unix-like environments, explore Android internals, build custom ROMs and kernels for personal use, and run experiments on my own machines. I enjoy exploring bleeding-edge ideas, but I do so cautiously, prioritizing safety and correctness.
